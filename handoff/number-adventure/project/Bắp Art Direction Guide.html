Bắp Number Adventure

Art Direction & Illustration Guide — Character, worlds, props, and visual system

Core Principles

1 · Warmth Over Flash

Illustrations should feel like a cozy picture book, not a flashy cartoon. Soft textures, muted vibrancy, gentle lighting. A child should feel safe and curious.

2 · Support, Never Distract

During gameplay, illustration serves the task. Backgrounds stay quiet. Props are clear and countable. Decoration lives in transitions, rewards, and world maps — never competing with the learning moment.

3 · One Character, Many Moods

Bắp is the single mascot across all worlds. Worlds are distinguished by color, environment props, and ambient elements — not by introducing new characters. This keeps the emotional bond strong and the system scalable.

4 · Every World Feels Different

Each world has its own dominant color, terrain shape language, and set of ambient objects. A child should know which world they're in from background alone, even with all UI removed.

5 · Parent Mode Is Calm

Parent-facing screens drop the playfulness to 10%. Illustration is minimal — small mascot avatars, subtle accents. Typography and whitespace do the work. Tone shifts from picture-book to clean editorial.

Illustration Style

Visual DNA

AttributeDirection
Overall feelHand-drawn storybook meets flat vector — organic but clean
ShapesRounded, blobby, organic. No sharp corners. Think bean shapes, not rectangles
OutlinesConsistent 3–4px dark stroke (#2D3A2E), slightly rounded endpoints
FillsFlat color with 1–2 subtle inner shadow layers for depth. No gradients on characters
TextureOptional grain overlay on backgrounds only (noise at 3–5% opacity). Characters stay clean
ShadowsSimple elliptical ground shadows. No drop shadows on characters
ScaleBắp is always the biggest character element on screen. Props are proportional to Bắp
PerspectiveFlat / slightly ¾ view. No strong perspective or vanishing points
Detail levelLow. 3–5 elements per illustration. Negative space is your friend

✓ Do

  • Use soft, rounded corners everywhere
  • Keep stroke weight consistent (3.5px at base scale)
  • Let backgrounds breathe with space
  • Use the dark ink color (#2D3A2E) for all outlines
  • Add rosy cheeks to any character/creature
  • Keep props countable and visually distinct

✗ Don't

  • Use sharp angles or pointed shapes
  • Add heavy textures or photorealistic elements
  • Use more than 4 colors per illustration
  • Draw scary, sad, or aggressive expressions
  • Add visual clutter during gameplay screens
  • Use thin hairline strokes

Color Usage Rules

  • Backgrounds: Use the lightest tint of the world's primary color. Radial gradient from tinted to cream.
  • Characters: Flat fill from primary palette. No gradients.
  • Outlines: Always #2D3A2E (ink). Never colored outlines.
  • Accents: Use a contrasting palette color sparingly — e.g. coral cheeks on a sun-colored Bắp.
  • 3 color max per scene: One dominant, one supporting, one accent. Plus ink for outlines.

Bắp Mascot

Character Sheet

Bắp is a small corn kernel / sprout character — round-bodied with two leaf sprouts on top. The name "Bắp" means corn in Vietnamese. The character is gender-neutral, species-ambiguous (part plant, part creature), and always approachable.

AttributeSpecification
Body shapeRounded kernel form — wider at bottom, narrower at top. Slightly egg-like. Drawn with a single bezier path.
Sprout leavesTwo asymmetric leaves on top — one leaning left (lighter sage), one leaning right (darker sage). Gives a sprouting/growing metaphor.
Face positionEyes at 55% height, mouth at 70% height. Face lives in the upper-middle of the body.
EyesSimple filled ellipses with a small white highlight dot. Never outlined — they're solid dark (#2D3A2E).
MouthSmall and expressive. Red-brown fill (#C14A2A) with ink outline. Changes dramatically by mood.
CheeksTwo circles at face sides, coral (#FFA48C) at 70% opacity. Always present in every mood.
Body textureSubtle horizontal curved lines across the body at 35% opacity — suggesting kernel rows. These are a signature detail.
Ground shadowEllipse below body, ink at 18% opacity. Anchors the character spatially.
LimbsNone. Bắp doesn't have arms or legs. It bobs and bounces to express movement.
Default size120×140px SVG viewbox. Scales cleanly from 32px (avatar chip) to 180px (welcome hero).

Why No Limbs?

Keeping Bắp limbless makes the character simpler to draw consistently, easier to animate (bob, bounce, wiggle), and more like a natural sprout growing from the ground. Expression comes entirely from face and body motion.

Expressions & Moods

Bắp has 5 core moods. Each is defined by eye shape and mouth shape only — body and cheeks stay the same. All expressions must be achievable with simple SVG path changes.

MoodEyesMouthWhen to Use
HappyOpen ellipses with highlight dotUpturned curve, open with red fillDefault idle state, home screen, greeting
WinkOne open, one curved-shut arcGentle smile, smaller openingWelcome screen, hints, playful moments
ThinkSmaller circles (focused look)Small "o" ellipseProfile age question, waiting for answer
CelebrateUpward happy arcs (squinting with joy)Wide open grinCorrect answer, reward screen, unlocks
SleepDownward peaceful arcsGentle closed smileTime limit reached, rest screen, quiet hours

Animation Behaviors

  • Idle: Gentle vertical bob — 6px up/down over 2.8s, ease-in-out, with a subtle 2° rotation
  • Celebrate: Quick scale pop (0.6 → 1.08 → 1.0) over 400ms
  • Wrong answer: Side-to-side wiggle (±4° rotation) over 500ms — sympathetic, not scolding
  • Transition: Slide-up from 24px below with fade, 300ms

World System

  • Color: One dominant palette color per world
  • Terrain: Unique shape language for ground, horizon, and structures
  • Props: 4–6 ambient objects unique to that world

Background Construction

Every world background uses the same 3-layer structure:

  1. Sky layer — Radial gradient from world's lightest tint (top) to cream (bottom). No clouds unless world-specific.
  2. Terrain layer — Organic blob shapes at the bottom 30–40%. Uses the world's mid-tone color.
  3. Ambient layer — Floating sparkles, leaves, bubbles, etc. Semi-transparent, gently animated. Never more than 8–12 particles.

✓ Prop Rules

  • Every countable object must be visually identical to its siblings
  • Minimum tap target: 44×44px on mobile
  • Props use the same 3.5px ink outline as everything else
  • Correct state: sage green glow. Wrong state: warm coral wobble.
  • Always include a tactile "bottom shadow" edge on tappable elements

✗ Avoid

  • Props that look similar but mean different things
  • Decorative elements near interactive props
  • Mixing counting objects within a single question
  • Props smaller than 14px at any screen size

Stickers & Collectibles

Sticker Design System

Stickers are earned by completing levels with 3 stars. They live in the Sticker Book — a collection screen. Each sticker is a self-contained mini illustration that follows the same style rules as the rest of the app.

AttributeSpecification
SizeSquare aspect ratio. Drawn at 80×80px, displayed at 40–60px in the grid.
ContainerRounded square (border-radius: 18px). Cream background (#FFF8EC). Subtle bottom shadow.
ContentA single object or creature from the current world. Simple, iconic, one subject per sticker.
Locked stateDashed border, no fill. A centered "?" in faint ink. No preview of the sticker.
Earn animationPop-in (scale 0.6 → 1.08 → 1.0). Staggered by 20ms per sticker in the collection grid.
Total count40 stickers across all 5 worlds (8 per world). Progress bar tracks collection.

Sticker Subjects by World

  • Number Forest: Oak tree, Mushroom, Tulip, Butterfly, Bee, Ladybug, Apple, Sunflower
  • Tens Town: House, Mailbox, Street lamp, Bench, Pot plant, Weather vane, Clock, Cat
  • Even/Odd Bridge: Lily pad, Fish, Paper boat, Stone, Reed, Frog, Dragonfly, Lantern
  • Math Kitchen: Apple pie, Cookie, Spoon, Jar, Oven mitt, Cupcake, Bowl, Whisk
  • Big Number Castle: Crown, Shield, Key, Gem, Flag, Treasure chest, Tower, Star medal

Sticker Illustration Rules

Each sticker should be recognizable at 40px. That means: one subject, centered, filling 70% of the frame. No background detail within the sticker. Use the same ink outline and flat fill style. Faces (on creatures) should always be happy/neutral — never sad or scary.

Reward Elements

Celebration System

Reward moments are the most illustratively rich screens in the app. This is where decoration is encouraged — confetti, sparkles, large mascot, bright colors.

ElementSpecification
Stars (1–3)Standard 5-point star shape. Gold (#FFD36E) filled with ink outline. Earned stars get a golden drop shadow (#B87C0E). Unearned stars are outlined only at 25% opacity. Pop-in animation staggered by 150ms.
Confetti30–40 small shapes falling from top. Mix of circles and rounded rectangles. Uses all 6 palette colors. Falls over 2–3 seconds with rotation. Each piece: 8–16px.
Sparkles4-point star shapes (diamond rotated). Gold (#FFB84A). 10–14 particles floating with scale-rotate animation. Duration: 2–4 seconds, looping.
CoinsCircle with a subtle inner "C" mark. Gold fill. Displayed as "+N" count, not individually animated. Simple and not over-emphasized.
Bắp moodAlways "celebrate" expression on reward screen. 140px size (hero). Bobble animation.
BackgroundSun variant gradient — brightest, warmest. Reward screens always feel golden and glowing.

Reward Intensity by Performance

  • 3 stars: Full confetti burst (40 pieces) + 14 sparkles + sticker earn animation if applicable. Maximum celebration.
  • 2 stars: Light confetti (20 pieces) + 8 sparkles. Still celebratory, just quieter.
  • 1 star: Sparkles only (6). No confetti. Bắp still celebrates — the message is "you finished, that's great." Never feels like a lesser reward.

✓ Reward Principles

  • Every completion is celebrated — there is no "fail" screen
  • Vary intensity, not positivity
  • Confetti colors use the full palette — the most colorful moment in the app
  • Sound design (out of scope here) should match intensity level

✗ Avoid

  • Greyed-out or empty stars that feel punitive
  • Comparison with other players or sessions
  • Excessive animation that delays the child from continuing
  • Reward screens that take longer than 3 seconds to fully animate in

The Handoff Moment

When a parent opens the parent gate, the visual tone shifts immediately. The background goes from warm gradient to flat gray-cream. Typography switches from Fredoka to Be Vietnam Pro. The mascot shrinks from hero to avatar. This contrast is intentional — it signals "you are now in the grown-up space" and gives parents confidence they're seeing real data, not a toy interface.

Where Illustration Appears in Parent Mode

  • Child profile card: Small BapMini avatar (32–42px) in a cream circle. Static.
  • Weekly summary card: Subtle sage gradient background — the only "warm" moment. No illustration, just color.
  • Tip/recommendation card: Light parchment background with a small 💡 emoji. No illustration.
  • Data charts: Use palette colors for bar fills — sage for goal-met days, sun for partial, cream for missed. This is the primary "color expression" in parent mode.
