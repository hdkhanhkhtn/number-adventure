# Art Direction: Mascot Bắp & World Themes

## Brand Character: Bắp

### Personality
- Warm and supportive companion
- Playful but not hyperactive
- Emotionally intelligent (responds to child's performance)
- Magical and whimsical
- Always encouraging, never scary

### Visual Characteristics
- Rounded, soft forms (no sharp edges)
- Expressive eyes and gentle facial expressions
- Approachable scale (feels like a friend, not overwhelming)
- Cute but not "baby-like" (respect age 4+ audience)
- Consistent pose and proportions across all screens

### Mascot Placement Guidelines

| Screen | Placement |
|--------|-----------|
| Onboarding | Center or prominent (introduce the friend) |
| Game screens | Corner or speech bubble (guides without dominating) |
| Correct answer | Animated jump/celebration |
| Encouragement (wrong answer) | Supportive pose, thumbs up, nod |
| Rewards/celebration | Full screen or large (celebratory moment) |
| Parent dashboard | Optional, very subtle (not distracting parents) |

### Emotional States to Illustrate
- Happy/celebratory
- Encouraging/supportive
- Thinking/guiding
- Surprised/excited
- Proud/proud of child

## World Themes

Each world has distinct visual identity while maintaining brand coherence.

### 1. Number Forest
**Mood:** Magical, cozy, nature-inspired
**Colors:** Sage greens, soft forest tones, warm cream accents
**Elements:** Trees, flowers, nature creatures, sunny glades
**Usage:** Introduces number recognition (1-20)
**Visual metaphors:** Paths, stepping stones, forest clearings

### 2. Tens Town
**Mood:** Bustling, organized, town-like
**Colors:** Bright sky blue, sunny yellows, warm brick tones
**Elements:** Houses, shops, streets, friendly buildings
**Usage:** Tens and ones, understanding place value
**Visual metaphors:** Building blocks, organized rows, town squares

### 3. Big Number Castle
**Mood:** Grand, magical, adventure-filled
**Colors:** Rich purples, golds, cool stone grays, lavender accents
**Elements:** Castle, towers, magical auras, treasure
**Usage:** Hundreds and thousands, larger numbers
**Visual metaphors:** Castle rooms, towers stacked, treasure chests

### 4. Even/Odd Bridge
**Mood:** Balanced, symmetrical, playful contrast
**Colors:** Two distinct color families (e.g., warm coral + cool lavender)
**Elements:** Bridge, two sides, balanced scales, paired objects
**Usage:** Even/odd classification
**Visual metaphors:** Two houses, scales, bridge dividing line

### 5. Math Kitchen
**Mood:** Cozy, warm, welcoming
**Colors:** Warm cream, soft corals, gentle oranges
**Elements:** Kitchen, food, pots, spoons, cooking tools
**Usage:** Addition and subtraction
**Visual metaphors:** Mixing bowl, recipe, ingredients, cooking

## Illustration Style

**Overall Aesthetic:**
- **Approach:** Playful, modern, child-friendly
- **Simplicity:** Simple shapes, clear forms (easy to recognize)
- **Line work:** Soft outlines, rounded corners
- **Color:** Saturated but soft (not neon); harmonious palette
- **Detail:** Minimal but intentional (avoid visual noise)
- **Proportion:** Friendly, exaggerated (big heads, expressive eyes)

**Style Attributes:**
- Simple geometric base shapes
- Soft, rounded forms throughout
- Gentle facial expressions
- Warm, approachable color palette
- No harsh shadows or high contrast (keep it friendly)
- Hand-drawn or hand-drawn-like feeling
- Suitable for UI integration (clear layers, easy to animate)

**Illustration Usage:**
- World backgrounds: Full-screen, immersive, sets mood
- Game props: Objects used in gameplay (blocks, numbers, food, etc.)
- Stickers & collectibles: Small, iconic, memorable
- Reward elements: Stars, badges, celebratory imagery
- Character expressions: Mascot Bắp in various emotional states
- Teaching visuals: Pairing illustrations, number visuals, process steps
